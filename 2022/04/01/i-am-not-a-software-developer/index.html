I am not a software engineer

Friday April 1, 2022 by 0xADADA

Software engineering, knowledge work, or most broadly any type of white collar labor is, primarily, the production of our own psychology. Our mental capacity is our means of production. We embody the values of our work. Our identity is tightly bound up with our careers, knowledge, skills, methods, company, and our industry‚Äîwe are our work.

Having our identities bound to our work, and most dangerously, to an employer severely limits ones ability to negotiate wages when that identity is immediately laid bare in monetary value‚Äîour human value is our wage.

Beyond wages, this mindset limits the horizons of what is possible to do with our own lived time.

I have put all my eggs in one basket. Identifying who we are with what we do is a cognitive limit.

I do software engineering. I am not a software engineer.
